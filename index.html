Product logic
Decision sequence
1
Market regime detection first
▼
Before any action, the system reads the current market environment. Trend direction, VIX level, and momentum bias are set. This context shapes every recommendation downstream — a bull regime gets different advice than a bear or neutral one.
VIX Analysis Trend Detection Momentum Bias
2
Portfolio diagnosis — concentration, risk, classification
▼
Your holdings are parsed and classified into Core, Growth, Tactical, and Speculative buckets. The system scores concentration risk, checks allocation against institutional targets, and flags positions that need attention before anything else moves.
Health Score Risk Flags Classification Concentration Check
3
Clear action plans with specific tickers and allocations
▼
The intelligence panel generates a concrete action plan: what to trim, what to add, target percentages, and the reasoning behind each move. Not vague guidance — specific tickers, specific weights, sequenced by priority.
Buy Signals Trim Signals Target Weights
4
Three ways forward — conservative, balanced, aggressive
▼
Every deployment plan comes in three risk postures. Conservative anchors in index and dividend positions. Balanced blends quality compounders with core holdings. Aggressive concentrates in high-conviction secular growth. Same analysis, three different conviction levels.
Conservative Balanced Aggressive

Deep drill-down only when needed
▼
Individual stock analysis, head-to-head comparisons, and watchlist verdicts are available on demand — not pushed at you constantly. When you need to go deep on a specific name, the full institutional-grade breakdown is one click away.

Architecture · Prototype → SaaS path

What moves where when this becomes a product

Stays client-side
• Visual layer & panels
• Chart rendering (Chart.js)
• Manual holdings entry
• Keyboard / drag-drop UX
• Color mode toggle
• Cadence projections (pure math)
Moves to backend
• Anthropic API proxy (hide key)
• Finnhub / market data proxy
• Screenshot OCR extraction
• AI prompt templating
• Rate limiting & caching
• Auth, sessions, billing
Becomes DB-backed
• User portfolios & history
• Watchlists & verdicts
• Strategy selections
• Feedback submissions
• Generated reports
• Audit trail of AI runs
Today the Anthropic and Finnhub keys ship to the browser — that is acceptable for a private prototype but must not ship to real users. The right production shape: a thin Node/Go/Python API gateway that signs every upstream call with server-held keys, enforces rate limits per authenticated user, and writes portfolio / verdict / strategy state to Postgres. The client then becomes a pure view layer calling /api/* with a session cookie, and no framework migration is required at that point — this single-file HTML can be carved into small components on the same day the backend ships.
